Projects

  • Genetics of non-cognitive skills

    What matter for educational success, other than intelligence? We looked at genetic variants associated with educational attainment without being associated with cognitive skills, to obtain a genome-wide association study (GWAS) of "non-cognitive" aspects of educational attainment. To do so, we ran a GWAS-by-subtraction with Genomic SEM. The GWAS allowed us to investigate the biology of non-cognitive skills and their genetic associations with socio-economic, behavioural and health traits.

    Here you can find the preprints on BiorXiv and on HCEO (latest), the FAQ, the tutorial to run your own GWAS-by-subtraction, the code used to run the analyses, and the summary statistics for the GWAS of non-cognitive and cognitive skills.
    You can also watch Margherita Malanchini presenting her work on "Educational success beyond cognitive ability: A genetic perspective", including our work on the genetics of non-cognitive skills.
