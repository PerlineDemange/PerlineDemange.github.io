Projects

  • Genetics of non-cognitive skills

    What matters for educational success, other than intelligence? We looked at genetic variants associated with educational attainment without being associated with cognitive skills, to obtain a genome-wide association study (GWAS) of "non-cognitive" aspects of educational attainment. To do so, we ran a GWAS-by-subtraction with Genomic SEM. The GWAS allowed us to investigate the biology of non-cognitive skills and their genetic associations with socio-economic, behavioural and health traits.

    This project is now published in Nature Genetics (see also the open access link and BioRxiv preprint). Here you can find the FAQ, the tutorial to run your own GWAS-by-subtraction, the code used to run the analyses, and the summary statistics for the GWAS of non-cognitive and cognitive skills.
    You can also watch the 15-min talk I gave on this project at EARLI 2021.

  • Indirect genetic effects of cognitive and non-cognitive skills

    Understanding how parents shape their children's educational trajectories is a socially important research goal. In this study, we used genetics to assess non-cognitive skills and to index environmental effects of parents, while controlling for direct genetic effects. We estimated indirect genetic effects (environmentally mediated effects of polygenic scores) in three european cohorts, using three analytic designs. Our findings stress the importance of cognitive and non-cognitive aspects of the home environment, and highlight differences in the analytic designs.

    This project is now published in Nature Communications. You can find the code used to run the analyses and simulations, and the pre-registration as well as slides of conference talks and posters on our OSF page.

    We simulated the three analytic designs to compare their biases, you can watch my 5-min summary here, as well as a two-minute primer on the study.
